# Impeccable — design context

Captured for **Portfolio Second** (Bryan X. Mendez). Update this file when positioning or visual strategy changes.

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary:** Hiring managers, creative directors, and recruiters evaluating Bryan for **full-time or in-house** design roles—often skimming quickly on a laptop during the workday, sometimes on a phone between meetings. They need fast proof of craft, range, and reliability.

**Secondary:** Fellow designers and collaborators assessing taste and process; occasional freelance or project inquiries (supported in the UI, but not the main optimization).

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** **intentional**, **typographic**, **systematic**.

Voice and emotional goals: confident clarity over noise; warmth from structured pastels and human copy, not from ornamental fluff. The site should feel like a serious designer who cares about grids, hierarchy, and packaging-level detail—never generic “template portfolio” energy.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **North star:** Swiss-inspired **neo-grid** discipline with **pastel tile** surfaces (peach, mint, sky) as sectional rhythm, **ink** typography, and a disciplined **signal** accent for emphasis and primary actions (`--paper` / `--ink` / `--signal` system in `app/assets/css/main.css`).
- **Typography:** Geist for the primary sans stack; Fraunces available for serif texture; Geist Mono for label / mono rhythm—loaded globally via `@nuxt/fonts` in `nuxt.config.ts`. Marketing-scale headings use a modular, fluid display treatment; UI stays legible and restrained.
- **Theme:** **Light and dark** with explicit user control (`useTheme`); light is the default reading mode; dark supports low-light viewing and preference.
- **Motion:** Scroll-linked reveals and imagery are part of the experience but must stay **state-driven**, not gratuitous; honor **`prefers-reduced-motion`** everywhere animations exist.
- **Anti-patterns (house + product):** Avoid cyan–purple “AI startup” gradients, decorative **gradient-filled text**, heavy glassmorphism, and **thick colored left/right stripes** on cards or callouts. Prefer full borders, surface tints, or typographic emphasis instead.

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity before cleverness:** Each major block should communicate role, scope, and proof within seconds—especially for busy hiring audiences.
2. **Typography as identity:** Type scale, weight, tracking, and line-breaking are primary design surfaces; micro-typographic fixes (glue, orphans) are on-brand, not optional polish.
3. **Restrained accent discipline:** Pastels carry most of the visual field; the signal hue earns its keep by staying relatively rare so CTAs and emphasis remain legible and memorable.
4. **True-to-life narrative:** Case studies, about copy, and structured data should stay aligned with Bryan’s real trajectory (Cal State Northridge / IntersectLA, NAXA brand leadership, retail and packaging breadth).
5. **Pragmatic accessibility:** Ship keyboard-safe navigation, visible focus, semantic landmarks, meaningful image alternatives, and motion-safe fallbacks—aim for inclusive defaults **without** claiming a formal WCAG conformance level unless you later choose to audit and commit to one.
